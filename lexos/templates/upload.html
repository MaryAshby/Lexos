About

Lexos is developed by the Lexomics Research Group at Wheaton College. It is used for transforming, analyzing, and visualizing texts. Lexos is designed for use primarily with small-to-medium-sized text collections, including texts written in ancient languages and languages that do not use the Latin alphabet. Lexos was created as an entry-level platform for Humanities scholars and students new to computational techniques, but is sophisticated enough for advanced research. The Lexos help manual can be accessed by clicking the "Show Help" button at the bottom left of the window. The help panel can be accessed from any page and will display information on how to use the page it was opened on.
